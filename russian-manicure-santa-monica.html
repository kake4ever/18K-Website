What Is a Russian Manicure?

Precision cuticle work, flawless results

A Russian manicure, also known as a dry or hardware manicure, uses an electric file (e-file) to meticulously remove the cuticle and dead skin around the nail plate without water or soaking. The technique originates from Eastern European nail artistry and is known for delivering the cleanest, most precise cuticle work possible.

Because the cuticle area is cleaned so thoroughly, gel polish adheres directly to the nail plate without any lifted edges or lifting at the base. The result is a manicure that looks flawless for longer — often 4 to 5 weeks — compared to 2 to 3 weeks with a standard gel manicure.

At 18K Nail Boutique in Santa Monica, our technicians are trained in the Russian manicure technique and use medical-grade e-file bits that are properly sanitized between every client.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a Russian manicure safe?
Yes, when performed by a trained technician. At 18K, we use properly sanitized e-file bits and follow strict protocols to ensure a safe, clean service every time.
Does a Russian manicure hurt?
It should not be painful. A skilled technician works carefully around the cuticle area. Some clients feel mild sensitivity, but it should never be uncomfortable.
How often should I get a Russian manicure?
Every 4 to 5 weeks, depending on your nail growth. Because the results last longer, many clients find they need fewer appointments than with a standard manicure.
Can I get nail art with a Russian manicure?
Absolutely. The clean, precise base created by a Russian manicure is perfect for any gel color, nail art, or design work.
